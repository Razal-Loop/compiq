Why ComplDoc exists

A documentation practice, built for the gap between the source text and the finished submission.

A regulation, a tender brief, and an aviation authority standard all reward the same thing — a document that maps precisely onto what the reader is checking for. ComplDoc reads the source text directly and builds evidence from it, whether the reader is an auditor, a bid evaluator, or an inspector.

Documentation architect, not lawyer — and not a proposal template mill either.

ComplDoc was built on a simple observation: whether the reader is an auditor checking a regulation, an evaluator scoring a tender, or an inspector reviewing an aviation manual, the underlying work is the same — reading a source text precisely and building a document that answers it point by point. That work gets outsourced, delayed, or handed to whoever on the team had time, when it deserves someone whose whole job is doing it well.

An engineering background means technical systems — AI architectures, ICT frameworks, aircraft maintenance procedures — are read directly rather than second-hand. A documentation background means the output holds together as something the reader can actually use: structured, cited, and consistent, not a narrative summary that raises more questions than it answers.

Every document ComplDoc produces is built from the primary source — the regulation text, the tender brief, or the civil aviation authority standard — not from secondary summaries or generic templates repackaged for a fee.

How an engagement runs

Four stages, in order, every time.

Stage 01

Read

The governing text is reviewed first — the regulation, the tender brief, or the aviation authority standard — to establish exactly what the finished document is being measured against.

Stage 02

Scope

Requirements are mapped against what already exists, producing a gap analysis or compliance matrix and a fixed quote for the work that closes it.

Stage 03

Draft

The document is written section by section, with client review checkpoints, so the final file reflects the system, submission, or operation as it actually is rather than a generic assumption of it.

Stage 04

Maintain or resubmit

For documents that need updating after the fact — a system that retrains, a manual amendment, a recurring grant cycle — the work moves onto a maintenance or resubmission schedule.

What governs the work

Three commitments that don't move, regardless of the engagement.
